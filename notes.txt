Banks are most negatively impacted by loans predicted as fully paid (class 0) when they actually default (class 1).
These cases are false negatives (FN), and they represent direct financial losses. From a business perspective, 
FN errors are the most critical, so the model should aim to minimize FN by increasing recall for the default class.

However, banks are also affected by loans predicted as charged off (class 1) when they are actually fully paid (class 0).
These are false positives (FP), which lead to rejecting good borrowers and therefore losing potential profit.

In summary, the model should balance both types of errors, with greater emphasis on reducing FN. Therefore, 
we fine-tune the model using the F2-score, which prioritizes recall over precision while still accounting for FP.
